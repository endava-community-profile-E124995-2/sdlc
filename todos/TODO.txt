# AI Act Validation Workflow — Implementation Plan

## 1) Objective

Build an internal workflow that:

* captures project details,
* determines whether a use case falls under the EU AI Act,
* maps the project to the relevant obligations,
* creates an audit trail,
* and routes ambiguous or high-risk cases to human review.

The legal baseline should be the **Official Journal version** of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 via ELI. For day-to-day usability, you can also use the EUR-Lex consolidated text, but EUR-Lex explicitly states consolidated texts are documentation tools and **do not have legal effect**. The AI Act entered into force on **1 August 2024** and applies progressively, with full roll-out foreseen by **2 August 2027**. ([EUR-Lex][1])

## 2) Target operating model

### Core principle

Position the workflow as **compliance decision support**, not as an automated legal certification engine.

### Source hierarchy

Use this hierarchy in the platform:

1. **Authoritative legal source:** Official Journal AI Act text via ELI
2. **Authoritative updates:** corrigenda to the AI Act
3. **Operational convenience:** consolidated EUR-Lex text
4. **Interpretation layer:** European Commission guidance
5. **Implementation support:** AI Act Service Desk / FAQs
6. **Technical conformity layer:** harmonised standards where available

That hierarchy matters because the AI Act is the law, the Commission guidance helps interpret grey areas like the **AI system definition** and **prohibited practices**, the Service Desk provides official implementation support, and harmonised standards can support **presumption of conformity** for relevant obligations. ([EUR-Lex][2])

## 3) Scope of the MVP

The first release should answer six business-critical questions:

1. Is this in scope as an **AI system**?
2. What is our role: **provider, deployer, importer, distributor, authorised representative**?
3. Is the use case potentially **prohibited**, **high-risk**, **transparency-only**, **GPAI-related**, or likely **out of scope**?
4. Which articles / annexes are relevant?
5. Which obligations are already applicable based on the current implementation timeline?
6. Does the case require **mandatory legal/compliance review**?

Given the progressive application of the AI Act, the workflow should be date-aware. It should already account for currently applicable requirements and be ready for broader obligations coming into application in **2026** and the remaining rollout through **2027**. ([European Commission][3])

## 4) Workstreams

## Workstream A — Legal and policy foundation

**Goal:** turn legal text into a maintainable rules model.

### Deliverables

* Source register for:

  * OJ ELI text
  * corrigenda
  * consolidated text
  * official guidance
  * Service Desk resources / FAQs
  * standards references
* Obligation taxonomy:

  * role
  * system type
  * risk class
  * article / annex reference
  * trigger condition
  * evidence required
  * implementation date
  * review owner
* Escalation policy for ambiguous cases

### Success criteria

* every rule in the workflow links back to a legal or official interpretive source
* every obligation has an owner and evidence expectation
* every unresolved interpretation is flagged, not hidden

## Workstream B — Intake and classification design

**Goal:** collect just enough information to classify projects reliably.

### Required intake fields

* project name and sponsor
* business purpose
* AI functionality description
* who builds it vs who deploys it
* end users
* whether the system supports or makes decisions
* domain/use case
* data categories used
* use of biometric or profiling elements
* integration with third-party AI models
* EU deployment / market placement
* human oversight model
* existing security / privacy controls

### Classification outputs

* in scope / out of scope
* role under the Act
* provisional risk category
* relevant articles and annexes
* implementation date sensitivity
* mandatory next actions
* escalation route

## Workstream C — Product and workflow build

**Goal:** operationalise the rules in a usable intake-to-decision flow.

### Core components

* project intake form
* rules engine
* evidence repository
* review queue
* decision log
* reporting dashboard
* legal source update monitor

### Workflow states

* Draft
* Submitted
* Auto-classified
* Needs review
* Approved with controls
* Blocked / prohibited
* Reassessment required

### Guardrails

* any prohibited-practice trigger forces escalation
* any high-risk signal requires compliance review
* low-confidence classification cannot auto-close
* source version used in the assessment must be recorded

## Workstream D — Governance and assurance

**Goal:** make the process defensible.

### RACI baseline

* **Executive sponsor:** AI governance owner
* **Product owner:** workflow accountability
* **Legal/compliance:** rule interpretation and sign-off
* **Privacy/DPO:** GDPR overlap
* **Security:** control validation
* **Engineering:** platform build and integrations
* **Internal audit / risk:** periodic assurance

### Governance controls

* monthly legal source review
* quarterly rule-set refresh
* mandatory reassessment on material model/use-case change
* annual control effectiveness review
* issue log for disputed interpretations

### C. What requires mandatory review

Examples:

* biometric-related use
* employment / HR decisions
* education assessment
* essential services
* law enforcement / public authority use
* GPAI integration with unclear provider/deployer responsibilities

### D. What counts as sufficient evidence

For every classification, require:

* business description
* system architecture summary
* model/vendor details
* data categories
* intended use
* human oversight details
* justification for classification result

## 8) KPIs

Track these from day one:

* classification turnaround time
* % of cases auto-classified
* % routed to manual review
* false positive / false negative rate from pilot checks
* % assessments with complete evidence
* number of reassessments triggered by material change
* lag between source update and rule-set update
* count of blocked / prohibited cases

## 9) Risks and mitigation

### Risk: false confidence

**Mitigation:** label outputs as decision support; require human review for ambiguous or sensitive cases.

### Risk: stale legal logic

**Mitigation:** monthly source review and version-controlled rules.

### Risk: over-classifying simple automation as AI

**Mitigation:** use the official AI system definition guidance.

### Risk: underestimating high-risk exposure

**Mitigation:** force Annex/use-case and role-based checks.

### Risk: no auditability

**Mitigation:** store source version, evidence, approver, rationale, and timestamp for every decision.

## 10) Executive recommendation

Run this as a **12-week governance-product build**, not as a legal side project. The smart play is:

* **OJ ELI text** as the legal anchor,
* **corrigenda + guidance + Service Desk + standards** as the maintenance layer,
* **workflow automation** for scale,
* **human review** for edge cases and sensitive uses.
