## Output Contracts

- `Product strategy / strategy brief`: problem, target segment, desired outcome, strategic rationale, options considered, recommendation, major risks, and success metric.
- `Discovery brief`: hypothesis, research question, target participant or source, evidence plan, assumptions, and decision the work is meant to inform.
- `Research synthesis`: observed patterns, evidence strength, pain points, unmet needs, contradictions, and implications for scope or prioritization.
- `PRD / BRD`: problem, goals, users or actors, scope, business rules, functional requirements, non-functional requirements, dependencies, risks, metrics, and open questions.
- `User story`: actor, need, outcome, scope notes, dependencies, and linked acceptance criteria.
- `Acceptance criteria set`: observable behavior, edge conditions, exclusions, success condition, and testability.
- `Decision memo`: decision, context, options, trade-offs, recommendation, rationale, risk, and follow-up actions.
- `Roadmap item / initiative`: problem or opportunity, expected outcome, timing assumption, dependency view, and sequencing rationale.
- `Launch brief`: audience, release scope, rollout approach, communication expectations, enablement needs, monitoring plan, and fallback expectations.

## Method-Selection Rules

- Use `JTBD` when the core question is user motivation, progress, and circumstance; use `persona` framing when segment differences matter more than job logic.
- Use a lightweight brief when scope is narrow and low-risk; use a `PRD / BRD` when the work crosses teams, introduces material complexity, or has governance implications.
- Use `BPMN` or state/decision-oriented modeling when rules, approvals, or branching behavior are important; use a simpler workflow map when the goal is shared understanding rather than formal precision.
- Use `RICE` when comparing option value under uncertainty with a strong reach component; use `WSJF` when sequencing is constrained by cost of delay and dependency flow.
- Use `user story mapping` when shaping a workflow or journey; use direct story splitting when the workflow is already understood and the problem is only delivery slicing.
- Use an experiment or prototype when the key uncertainty is user behavior or adoption; use delivery and readiness reviews when the uncertainty is operational or executional.
- Use a decision memo when stakeholders disagree or a recommendation needs explicit rationale; use a brief summary only when the decision is low-risk and locally contained.
