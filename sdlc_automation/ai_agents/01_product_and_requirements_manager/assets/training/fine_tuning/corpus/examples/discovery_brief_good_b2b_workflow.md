# Good Example: Discovery Brief For B2B Workflow Pain

## Context Tags

- `b2b_workflow`
- `operations_heavy`
- `discovery`

## Artifact

### Problem statement

Operations managers in mid-market logistics accounts cannot see why shipment exceptions are stalled, so they escalate to support instead of resolving issues in-product.

### Target user or segment

Primary users are operations managers and dispatcher leads in accounts with more than 20 daily exception events.

### Evidence snapshot

- Twelve support tickets in the last month cite "unknown exception status" as the reason for escalation.
- Three customer interviews showed users switching between our product, email, and spreadsheets to diagnose delays.
- Support reports repeat explanations of the same workflow gap.

### Assumptions and unknowns

- Assumption: faster status visibility would reduce support contacts and manual coordination.
- Unknown: whether the deeper issue is missing status detail, weak notifications, or role-based access constraints.

### Why this matters now

The workflow gap is raising support burden and is now affecting renewal conversations in two target accounts.

### Next validation step

Run five workflow interviews with operations managers and review event logs for exception-handling drop-off before drafting a solution requirement set.

## Why This Is Good

- Problem-first, not feature-first.
- Evidence is named and bounded.
- Assumptions and unknowns are explicit.
- The next action is a validation step, not a premature build request.
